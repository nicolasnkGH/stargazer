Notable comets currently in the inner solar system. Some are visible with binoculars or a small telescope.
🌠 C/2023 A3 Tsuchinshan-ATLAS Naked Eye
📐 Perihelion: Sep 27, 2024 · Closest approach: Oct 12, 2024
One of the brightest comets in decades — reached magnitude −4.9 at peak. Now fading but still a historic visitor. Expected to return in ~80,000 years.
🏎️ Speed: ~68 km/s 📡 Origin: Oort Cloud ⭐ Peak Mag: −4.9
🌠 12P/Pons-Brooks Binoculars
📐 Perihelion: Apr 21, 2024 · Period: ~71 years
The "Devil Comet" — famous for its cryo-volcanic outbursts that gave it distinctive horn-shaped coma. Last visible to the naked eye in 1954. Returns ~2095.
🏎️ Speed: ~44 km/s 📡 Short-period ⭐ Peak Mag: +4.0
🌠 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko Telescope
📐 Perihelion: ~Nov 2, 2021 · Period: ~6.5 years
The Rosetta Mission target. ESA's Rosetta spacecraft orbited it for 2 years and landed the Philae probe on its surface in 2014 — the first soft comet landing in history.
🏎️ Speed: ~34 km/s 📡 Jupiter-family 📏 Size: 4.3 × 2.6 km
🌠 C/2024 G3 (ATLAS) Jan 2025
📐 Perihelion: Jan 13, 2025 · Southern hemisphere favored
Reached potentially spectacular brightness (mag −2 to −3) near perihelion in January 2025, best seen from the southern hemisphere. Likely a dynamically new comet on its first inner solar system pass.
📡 Oort Cloud ⭐ Est. Peak: −3 🔭 Best: S. Hemisphere

About StarGazer

I created this application because getting started with stargazing can be overwhelming. There are too many apps with too much data.

I built this to focus on exactly what matters most for a beginner: knowing what's in the sky tonight, where to point the telescope, and nothing else.

🚫 No Ads 🔓 No Tricks ✨ Just Stars
